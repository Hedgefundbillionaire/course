Why Creandum: Interviews, Careers, & Portfolio

Last updated April 2025

Overview

Creandum is one of Europe's oldest and most consistent early-stage venture firms. Staffan Helgesson founded it in 2003 after a McKinsey stint in San Francisco, convinced that Europe could produce global technology leaders. The firm has proved him right repeatedly. Fund VII closed in 2024 at €500M — raised in just 12 weeks. Across all funds, roughly one in six seed investments has become a unicorn.

The portfolio includes some of Europe's most iconic companies. Creandum was an early backer of Spotify, which IPO'd in 2018 at $30B and is now worth $90B+. The firm also backed Klarna at seed — Klarna IPO'd on NYSE in 2025 at a $15B market cap. More recently, Creandum invested in Lovable and Trade Republic, both of which have reached unicorn status.

Creandum operates from Stockholm, London, Berlin, and San Francisco. The firm is genuinely pan-European in its sourcing — comfortable investing in Scandinavian enterprise software, German fintech, and French AI companies. The team of 34 is large enough to provide real portfolio support but small enough that every partner touches every deal.

Interview Process at Creandum

Creandum's process typically runs three rounds. Round 1 — a screening call with an associate. Expect questions about companies you admire, markets you find compelling, and your background. Candidates are sometimes asked to record a short video pitch of a company as part of initial screening — pick one you have thought deeply about.

Round 2 — a case study blending consulting-style market sizing with financial due diligence. You may be given a company to evaluate and asked to present your investment recommendation with numbers behind it. How big is the market? What's the competitive landscape? What would need to be true for this company to be worth €1B?

Round 3 — senior partner interview assessing fit and conviction. This round is less about analysis and more about whether you can hold your own in a room with experienced investors and opinionated founders.

Careers at Creandum

Creandum does not run formal on-cycle recruiting. Most junior hires come through direct outreach or referrals. The London office under Peter Specht focuses on AI and technical SaaS. The Berlin office under Simon Schmincke covers DACH fintech and marketplace businesses. If you are targeting Creandum, pick the office and the partner whose thesis aligns with your interests, and reach out directly with a specific view on a company or market.
