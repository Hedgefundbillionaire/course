Why Partech: Interviews, Careers, & Portfolio

Last updated April 2025

Overview

Partech is one of Europe's oldest and most global technology investors. Founded as Paribas Technologies in 1982, the firm became independent in 2008. Today it manages €2.5B across seed, venture, and growth funds with a portfolio spanning 220 companies across 40 countries. Partech is one of the few major European VCs with a dedicated Africa strategy — the Partech Africa fund has backed companies like Yassir (Algeria), Moove (Nigeria), and Wave (Senegal).

The firm operates from Paris, Berlin, San Francisco, and Dakar. In France, Partech is ranked #1 VC by CB Insights. The team has completed 21+ IPOs and 50+ M&A transactions above $100M. Recent portfolio highlights include Pennylane, Agicap, and Brevo.

What makes Partech distinctive is its combination of institutional scale and genuine global reach. Few European VCs can claim active investment teams across Europe, the US, and Africa. This geographic breadth gives Partech a sourcing advantage and allows it to support founders expanding internationally in ways that more regionally focused funds cannot.

Interview Process at Partech

Partech runs one of the more structured junior recruiting processes in European VC, particularly across its Paris and Berlin offices. The process runs three rounds:

Round 1 — Screening call with an associate covering your background, companies you find interesting, and your views on technology markets. Expect to be asked about a company you would invest in and one you would avoid.

Round 2 — Senior interview with a consulting-style market sizing case. You might be asked to estimate the TAM for a SaaS product in a specific European vertical, or to walk through how you would assess the competitive dynamics in a particular market.

Round 3 — Formal fit interview with a partner focused on investment conviction and cultural alignment. Partners are testing whether you have genuine passion for the work or are just checking a box on a finance career path.

Careers at Partech

Partech is one of the more active junior hirers in European VC. The Paris and Berlin offices are the primary entry points. The firm values candidates with language skills — French and German are particularly useful, as is Arabic for the Africa team. International experience and the ability to build rapport with founders across cultures is a genuine differentiator given Partech's global remit.
