Why Kima Ventures: Interviews, Careers, & Portfolio

Last updated April 2025

Overview

Kima Ventures is the world's most active early-stage investor. Backed by Xavier Niel — founder of Free, 42, and Station F — the firm invests in 2-3 startups per week, writing €150K cheques with no sector or geography restrictions. Since 2010 Kima has backed over 1,200 companies across 40+ countries with 21 unicorns. Unlike traditional VC funds, Kima does not raise from external LPs. Xavier Niel funds the firm directly, giving it unusual speed — investment decisions happen in days, not weeks.

The model is deliberately different from most VC firms. Kima is not looking for deep board involvement or 15-20% ownership stakes. Instead, the firm takes small positions early and relies on portfolio volume and follow-on rights to generate returns. This means Kima sees more deal flow than almost any other fund in Europe — a significant advantage for junior team members who want exposure to the full breadth of the startup ecosystem.

Jean de La Rochebrochard and Alexis Robert run the day-to-day investment operation. The team is small — four partners — but the deal velocity is enormous. Every week brings dozens of pitches, investment memos, and decisions. There is no other fund in Europe where you will see as many companies as quickly.

Interview Process at Kima

Kima actively hires juniors and screens for outliers over pedigree. Non-traditional backgrounds are welcomed — the team cares about how you think, not where you went to school.

Stage 1 — Written screening: short questions about why you want to work in venture, what you have done, and what excites you about early-stage investing. Keep it sharp. Generic answers about wanting to "work with innovative founders" will not pass.

Stage 2 — Investment memo exercise: Kima sends you four or five companies and asks which you would invest in and which you would not. Write brief investment memos for each — not deep financial models, but clear reasoning about the market, the team, and the risk-reward.

Stage 3 — Live interview with a GP who describes startups in real time and asks whether you would invest. This round tests how you think on your feet — speed and clarity matter more than arriving at the "right" answer.

Careers at Kima

Kima is one of the best training grounds in European VC precisely because of the deal volume. You will see more companies in a month at Kima than you would see in six months at most other funds. The team is small and junior hires are a genuine part of how the firm operates given the volume of deals it processes each week. If you want to develop pattern recognition fast, this is the place.
