Why Andreessen Horowitz (a16z): Interviews, Careers, & Portfolio

Last updated April 2025

Overview

Andreessen Horowitz is one of the most influential venture capital firms in the world. Marc Andreessen and Ben Horowitz founded it in 2009 with the thesis that software would eat the world — a prediction that has proved prescient across every industry. Today the firm manages $42B+ across dedicated funds spanning American Dynamism, Bio, Consumer, Crypto, Enterprise, Fintech, Games, and Infrastructure.

What makes a16z different from most VC firms is its platform model. The firm employs over 100 operators across marketing, recruiting, business development, regulatory affairs, and technical advisory. When a16z invests, portfolio companies gain access to this full team — not just the GP who led the deal. This model has been widely imitated but never fully replicated at a16z's scale.

For European candidates, a16z is relevant because the firm has been quietly expanding its European presence through portfolio company support and selective investments. The London office has grown, and the firm's crypto fund has been particularly active in European blockchain infrastructure. Understanding a16z's thesis and content output is valuable even if you ultimately work at a European fund.

Interview Process at a16z

a16z runs a structured multi-round process. Early rounds cover fit — your background, why venture, and what sectors you care about. Middle rounds involve technical or sector-specific discussions with GPs from the relevant fund. The final round is typically a case study where you evaluate a company and present an investment recommendation.

The firm tests for two things above all: genuine intellectual curiosity about technology and the ability to form contrarian views backed by evidence. a16z partners write extensively — if you cannot engage with their published thinking on a topic, you are not ready to interview.

Careers at a16z

a16z hires at Analyst, Associate, and Partner levels. Most junior hires come from top investment banks, consulting firms, or operators at portfolio companies. The firm also has a large platform team where non-investment hires support portfolio companies across recruiting, marketing, and go-to-market strategy.

For European candidates, the London office is the relevant entry point. Engaging with a16z content — particularly their sector-specific newsletters and the a16z podcast — is the minimum bar for demonstrating interest. The firm receives thousands of applications so standing out requires genuine, specific knowledge of their portfolio and investment thesis.
