Why Hummingbird Ventures: Interviews, Careers, & Portfolio

Last updated April 2025

Overview

Hummingbird Ventures bets on founders when it is not obvious. Founded by Barend Sears, the firm operates with no geographic or sector constraints — backing outlier founders globally from pre-seed through Series A. The team is deliberately small and partner-heavy, with each investment getting deep involvement from the partners.

The portfolio reflects this contrarian approach. Hummingbird was an early backer of Deliveroo (when food delivery was considered a low-margin commodity business), Kraken (when crypto exchanges were considered niche), and Lovable (an AI coding tool that reached unicorn status in 2025). The common thread is not a sector thesis but a pattern of finding founders who are building something that the consensus has not yet recognized.

Mario Gabriele — partner at Hummingbird and founder of The Generalist, one of the most widely read newsletters in venture — joined in 2023. His addition brought content creation and community building into the firm's DNA. The Generalist reaches hundreds of thousands of readers weekly and gives Hummingbird unusual visibility for a firm of its size.

Interview Process at Hummingbird

Hummingbird's process is relationship-driven. There is no formal case study or rigid interview sequence. Instead, expect multiple conversations with different partners over time, each exploring how you think about founders, markets, and non-consensus opportunities. The team is small enough that every hire is a significant decision.

Discussions focus on your past experience, how you evaluate founders with no revenue and no product, and your genuine views on where the market is wrong. Hummingbird is looking for people who can identify outliers — if you can only evaluate companies through conventional frameworks, this is not the right firm.

Careers at Hummingbird

Hummingbird does not publicly market junior positions. The team is small and hires through the network. Engage with their content — particularly The Generalist — reach out directly to partners, and build a point of view on the kind of outlier founders they back. If you can articulate a non-obvious investment thesis that the firm has not yet seen, you will get a conversation.
